The Aim of the Game

Welcome to the world of Minesweeper, a game of strategy and deduction where you must uncover hidden mines and navigate a treacherous grid without detonating any of these explosive hazards. Your goal is to identify and flag all the mines while revealing the safe squares, ultimately achieving victory by clearing the entire grid.

Gameplay Overview

Investigate: Start by selecting a square by entering its row and column numbers.

Reveal or Flag: If the square is safe, a numerical value will appear, indicating the number of mines surrounding it. If it's a mine, the game ends abruptly, and you're declared the loser.

Deduce and Flag: Utilize the revealed numbers to deduce the potential locations of hidden mines. If you're unsure about a square, enter "y" after selecting it to flag it as a suspected mine.

Strategy and Risk: Carefully consider your moves, balancing the need to uncover safe squares with the potential risk of detonating a mine. Flagging potential mines can guide your decisions and reduce the risk of mishaps.

Victory and Defeat: If you successfully flag all the mines while uncovering the safe squares, you emerge victorious. However, detonating a single mine results in your downfall, ending the game in defeat.

Inspiration

This game draws inspiration from the renowned classic Minesweeper, a staple of Windows operating systems for decades. The thrill of navigating a grid filled with unknown dangers and the satisfaction of clearing it unscathed have captivated players worldwide. Additionally, our implementation is heavily inspired by The Coding Train's "Coding Challenge #71: Minesweeper" video, which provides a comprehensive guide to creating this classic game.
